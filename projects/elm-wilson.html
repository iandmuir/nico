A new skyline for downtown Bethesda.

Three high-rise towers — two residential and one office — rise from a shared podium directly above the new Purple Line terminus. At 295 feet, they are the tallest buildings Bethesda permits, and they deliberately re-scale a district that had long been defined by mid-rise commercial stock.

Podium as city-maker

The shared podium stitches together the transit concourse, two retail levels, lobbies for three distinct towers, and a rooftop amenity deck — all while resolving the geometric clash between a diagonal rail alignment and the Wisconsin Avenue street grid above.

Role

Nicolas worked on the core-and-shell podium team from schematic design through construction documentation, coordinating the intersection of residential, office and transit systems.
